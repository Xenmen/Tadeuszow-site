This is the homepage of Daniel Tadeuszow, a recently situated resident of Victoria, BC. It is being built, bit by bit, in a manner similar to Dr Frankenstein's monster, so between paragraphs there may be sudden breaks/leaps in thought. This is a transitional condition, as I am building the site in my spare time, and must temporarily place speed over polish. Anyways, I hope you find lots of useful information, and sapient art!

Daniel's main objectives in life are reduction in recurring life costs, culminating in projects to address these three topics: Housing Costs, Food Costs, and Information Costs. Presently, the amount of information gathered on these topics is colossal, and not yet organized for easy reading. This will be addressed as these projects progress, reducing the time Daniel needs to work to provide his basic necessities, freeing up more time to document the path taken so that you may use and improve that information. If you have any information you feel would be useful to these projects, please consider sending Daniel an email, or modifying the website yourself and making a 'pull request' on github.


Daniel's background in technology is very varied. At the age of two, Daniel began using the family Atari ST to play a train simulation game. At age 20, Daniel was designing his own Nintendo DS software. In the years between, many experiences with machines and software of all kinds, and in the years since (Daniel is 23 at the time of writing this page), he has focused on web technology almost exclusively. This website is hand-crafted HTML5/Javascript, with the intention of gradually optimizing it. Additional side projects using Unity3D, Python and NodeJS are underway, and tutorials will be available as this software is made available.
