Smea Lum is the creator of Aperture Science DS and DSCraft, which are functional recreations of Portal and Minecraft with the limitation of having 4MB of RAM (main memory and video RAM). For perspective, that means both games run on about three floppy disks of active memory.

Smea's projects were made possible by the decade-long labours of Dave Murphy, who created a toolchain for Nintendo DS development and released it to the world at no charge.
